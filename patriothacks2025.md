# PatriotHacks Fall 2025 – Hackathon Overview

## About
**PatriotHacks 2025** is a 60-hour, fully online hackathon where college students from any major team up to build real solutions with a focus on **Startups** and **AI Integration**.  
No travel, no hassle — just building your best work.

**Dates:** Nov 14–16, 2025 (Build Window)  
**Deadline:** Nov 16, 2025 @ 10:00 p.m. ET  
**Kickoff Livestream:** Nov 14, 2025 @ 9:00 a.m. ET  
**Judging:** Nov 18–19, 2025 (asynchronous)  
**Results:** Nov 20–21, 2025 (livestream + announcements)  
**Format:** Fully online, asynchronous  
**Participation:** College students worldwide (solo or teams of 2–4 members)  

---

## Tracks
1. **Startup** — Venture-first ideas with clear users, value proposition, and path to market  
2. **AI Integration** — Smart, ethical AI applied to real-world problems  

---

## Requirements
All submissions must be uploaded to the official Devpost project portal by the deadline. One submission per team.

### 1) Project Page
Include:
- Project title & short tagline  
- Problem & target audience (the “why”)  
- Your solution (what you built and what it does)  
- How it works (system overview, user flow)  
- Tech stack (frameworks, APIs, models, datasets)  
- Challenges, learnings, and next steps  
- Team members (names, roles, school)  

### 2) Demo Video (2–3 minutes)
- Show the product in action (prototype or working demo)  
- Briefly explain problem, solution, and novelty  
- Upload to a shareable platform (unlisted link is fine)  

### 3) Code & Assets
- Public repo link (GitHub/GitLab) with clear README  
- Setup/run instructions  
- Architecture diagram or brief technical overview  
- Licenses for any third-party assets or datasets  
- If app isn’t easily runnable, provide a recorded flow or hosted demo link

## Judging Criteria
- Innovation & novelty of the solution  
- Real-world impact  
- Clarity of presentation  
- Quality and functionality of the final project  
- Effective teamwork (collaboration, brochure details, overall presentation)  

---

## Allowed Tools & Resources
- Public frameworks, APIs, AI tools, open-source libraries  
- AI-generated content (ChatGPT, Claude, Midjourney, etc.) if disclosed  
- No proprietary or paid data without permission  
- No sensitive personal data (PII, medical data without consent)  

---

## Prohibited Behavior
- Plagiarism or using someone else’s work  
- Using code from previous personal/class projects without disclosure  
- Using tools/datasets without legal access  
- Harassment, hate speech, discrimination, disruptive behavior  
- Bribing judges or rigging judging  
- Submitting malicious software/tools  

**Violation may result in disqualification**
